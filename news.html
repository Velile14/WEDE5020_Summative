August Adoption Drive: 42 Pets Found Homes!

Our biggest adoption month yet! Thanks to your support, dozens of animals are now in loving homes.

This year’s adoption drive exceeded every expectation. Families from all over Pretoria, Johannesburg, and Centurion came through our doors ready to open their hearts. Among the 42 adoptions were senior dogs, bonded pairs, and even a three-legged kitten named Miso—proof that every pet deserves a chance.

Volunteers worked tirelessly to prepare the animals, handle meet-and-greets, and conduct interviews. Many adopters shared emotional stories about why they chose to adopt—from filling the silence at home after losing a loved one, to giving their children a chance to learn compassion.

With more adoption events planned for the year, we’re excited to see even more happy endings.

Emergency Rescue: Midnight Mission Saves 5 Puppies

When we got the call, our team didn’t hesitate.

A community member alerted us about a litter abandoned near a construction site. With no lighting, dropping temperatures, and loud machinery nearby, the situation was urgent.

Our rescue team arrived armed with blankets, crates, and thermal pads. After carefully navigating debris and pipes, they discovered the puppies huddled together under a sheet of rusted metal. All five were cold, dehydrated, and infested with fleas.

Thanks to quick action, they were safely transported to our clinic, treated, fed, and warmed. Today, all five pups—Nova, Comet, Dune, Pebble, and Wisp—are recovering well in foster homes.

Their story is a reminder of why community reporting is essential.

Volunteer Spotlight: Meet Thandi, Our Foster Hero

From bottle-feeding kittens to organizing fundraisers, Thandi’s dedication inspires everyone.

Thandi joined Paw Haven Rescue in 2021 and has fostered more than 60 animals—many requiring round-the-clock care. Her home is always filled with warm blankets, feeding schedules, and enrichment toys.

“Every animal teaches you something new,” she says. “Patience, empathy, resilience—these pets give so much more than they take.”

When she’s not fostering, she assists with adoption interviews, organizes donation drives, and trains new volunteers. Her passion continues to uplift our entire rescue community.

From Street to Sanctuary: Leo’s Journey

Abandoned and injured, Leo’s transformation is a testament to resilience.

When Leo was found, he was limping, severely underweight, and terrified of people. It took weeks of gentle handling, medical care, and slow exposure therapy before he wagged his tail for the first time.

Today, Leo is almost unrecognizable—healthy, playful, and confident. His foster family describes him as “a giant teddy bear who loves belly rubs and naps in the sun.”

Leo’s journey represents the heart of Paw Haven: healing, hope, and a second chance.

Healing Through Adoption: A Family’s Story

When the Mokoena family adopted Bella, they found healing after loss.

After the passing of their grandmother, the family felt a void in their home. Bella, a shy rescue pup with big brown eyes, changed everything.

At first, she hid behind furniture and avoided touch. But with patience and kindness, she slowly warmed up—curling beside the children during homework time and greeting the parents at the door.

“Bella brought joy back into our home,” Mrs. Mokoena shared. “We didn’t rescue her—she rescued us.”
